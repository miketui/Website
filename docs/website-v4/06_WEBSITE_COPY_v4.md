## 5 starter blog posts
### 1. The Craft Is Real. So Is the Business.
Freelance beauty work can make you feel like the only thing that matters is the final image. The image matters. But so does the invoice, the call time, the kit fee, the boundary, and the recovery day after the job. This post opens the conversation the book keeps returning to: your talent deserves structure.

### 2. Pricing Is a Conversation With Your Future Self.
A rate is not just a number. It is a boundary around time, preparation, skill, transportation, tools, communication, and the energy it takes to show up ready. This post explains how to approach pricing without turning your artistry into apology.

### 3. Your Kit Is a Business System.
The kit is more than product. It is readiness. It is memory. It is the quiet confidence of knowing where everything lives and why it is there. This post frames kit-building as an operational practice, not just a shopping list.

### 4. The Calm Before Call Time.
Good preparation changes the way you enter a room. It helps you listen better, move cleaner, and protect the client experience. This post offers a short pre-job ritual for stylists who want less scramble and more presence.

### 5. You Can Grow Without Performing Panic.
Not every career move has to be loud. Some growth happens through clearer notes, better follow-up, cleaner boundaries, and honest reflection after the job. This post is a reminder that momentum can be measured in steadiness.
